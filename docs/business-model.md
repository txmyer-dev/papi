# The People's AI (PAPI) - Business & Mission Model

## 1. Mission & Vision

**Mission:** Democratize access to Large Language Models by aggregating the world's free inference capacity into a single, reliable, non-profit infrastructure.

**Vision:** A world where access to powerful AI is not determined by wealth, geography, or corporate gatekeeping, but by need and creativity.

**Core Principle:** We are not in the business of selling inference. We are in the business of providing **managed access to free infrastructure**.

---

## 2. Legal & Tax Strategy

### 2.1 Primary Entity: 501(c)(3) Non-Profit

**Why:** This is the optimal structure for this project because:

- **Grant Eligibility:** Opens doors to foundation grants from Ford, Omidyar, Mozilla, MacArthur, etc., that are explicitly restricted to 501(c)(3) organizations.
- **Tax-Deductibility for Donors:** Individual and corporate donors can write off their contributions, increasing the likelihood of donations.
- **Credibility:** Signals to the public and the community that this is a mission-driven project, not a cash-grab.
- **Focus:** Forces the organization to prioritize mission over profit, which aligns perfectly with the founder's intent.

**Action Item:** Consult with a non-profit attorney to file for 501(c)(3) status as soon as a basic operational budget is established.

### 2.2 Funding Sources (Priority Order)

| Priority | Source | Details |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Foundation Grants** | Apply to tech-for-good foundations. Focus on "digital equity" and "open infrastructure." |
| 2 | **Individual Donors** | Leverage the mission story to attract micro-donations from developers and supporters globally. |
| 3 | **Corporate Sponsorships** | Partner with companies that benefit from open AI ecosystems (e.g., cloud providers, open-source tool companies). |
| 4 | **Premium Subscriptions** | The $1,500/year managed service. This is a cost-recovery mechanism, not a profit center. |

## 3. The Service Model

### 3.1 Tier 1: Free (Community-Supported)

This is the core of the mission. It is funded by grants and donations.

**Access:**
- Access to the aggregated API via a shared pool.
- Subject to fair-use limits to ensure equitable distribution.
- Supported by a community forum (e.g., Discord).

**Who it's for:**
- Students in developing countries.
- Independent researchers.
- Non-profits building public-good applications.
- Developers building prototypes.

**Cost to User:** $0
**Cost to PAPI:** Minimal (server costs are shared across the free pool and covered by grants).

### 3.2 Tier 2: Premium Managed Service ($1,500/year)

This tier is for users who need a dedicated, always-on server without managing the software themselves. **The inference is always free; this fee is strictly for the service of hosting and maintaining the server.**

**Included:**
- **Always-On Dedicated Server:** A persistent instance running 24/7.
- **Automatic Security Updates:** Regular patching and security audits.
- **Automatic Model Updates:** New free models and providers are added as they become available.
- **Bring Your Own Keys (Optional):** Users can add their own API keys for even higher throughput.
- **Priority Support:** Faster response times via a dedicated support channel.

**Who it's for:**
- Small businesses that need reliability but can't afford proprietary API costs.
- Researchers who need consistent uptime for long-running jobs.
- Developers who want a "set it and forget it" solution.

**Cost to User:** $1,500/year
**Cost to PAPI:** ~$1,200-$1,800/year per server (depending on specs and provider).
**Margin:** ~0-20% (This is a cost-recovery model, not a profit center. Any surplus goes back into the free pool).

## 4. The "Free Inference" Narrative

This is the most critical part of the pitch. The messaging must be crystal clear.

### 4.1 The Problem (Proprietary AI is Expensive)
- OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic charge $20-$200+/month.
- This price locks out students, researchers, and non-profits in the Global South.
- Even "free" tiers are fragmented and hard to use.

### 4.2 The Solution (Aggregated Free Infrastructure)
- Every major AI lab offers a free tier.
- Stacked together, they provide ~1.7 billion tokens/month.
- We aggregate these into a single, reliable API.

### 4.3 The Pricing Logic (Service vs. Inference)
- **Inference:** We charge $0. We never mark up the cost of inference.
- **Service:** We charge $1,500/year to cover the cost of hosting, security, and maintenance of the managed servers.
- **Metaphor:** "We're like a public library for AI. The books (inference) are free. The library building (server) needs funding to stay open."

## 5. The Impact Thesis

This project is not just a tech startup; it is a **digital public good**.

**Target Beneficiaries:**
1. **Students & Education:** Enable AI-powered learning and research in under-resourced schools.
2. **Researchers:** Provide high-quality inference for scientific papers and data analysis without grant money going to API bills.
3. **Non-Profits:** Allow NGOs to automate operations, analyze data, and serve beneficiaries more effectively.
4. **Developers in the Global South:** Bridge the "AI divide" by providing the same tools to a developer in Lagos as to a developer in San Francisco.

**Measurable Outcomes (KPIs for Grants):**
- Number of free API requests served per month.
- Number of active users in the free tier.
- Number of premium subscriptions (serving as cost-recovery).
- Community growth (Discord members, GitHub stars).
- Case studies of users achieving specific goals (e.g., "Student in X built Y using PAPI").
